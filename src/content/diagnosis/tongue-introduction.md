## Why Tongue Diagnosis?<div class="my-6 rounded-lg overflow-hidden shadow-sm">
  <img src="/images/medical-anatomy.jpg" alt="Tongue and body connection in Traditional Chinese Medicine" class="w-full h-48 sm:h-64 object-cover"/>

For over 2,000 years, TCM practitioners have used tongue observation as a primary diagnostic tool. The tongue is considered a **map of the internal organs**, with different regions corresponding to different organ systems.

Tongue diagnosis is **non-invasive, immediate, and surprisingly accurate**.

## How to Examine Your Tongue

<div class="grid sm:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-green-50 border border-green-200 rounded-lg p-4">
    <p class="font-semibold text-green-800 text-sm mb-1">Do</p>
    <ul class="text-sm text-green-700 space-y-1">
      <li>✓ Examine in natural daylight</li>
      <li>✓ Check before brushing or eating</li>
      <li>✓ Morning is the best time</li>
      <li>✓ Relax your tongue naturally</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-red-50 border border-red-200 rounded-lg p-4">
    <p class="font-semibold text-red-800 text-sm mb-1">Don't</p>
    <ul class="text-sm text-red-700 space-y-1">
      <li>✗ Check after coffee, tea, or beets</li>
      <li>✗ Use artificial lighting alone</li>
      <li>✗ Scrape your tongue before checking</li>
      <li>✗ Stick your tongue out too hard</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## What Does a Healthy Tongue Look Like?

<div class="bg-primary-light rounded-lg p-5 my-6 border border-primary/20">
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <span class="text-3xl flex-shrink-0">✅</span>
    <div>
      <h4 class="font-semibold text-text-body">A healthy tongue is:</h4>
      <ul class="text-sm text-text-muted mt-2 space-y-1">
        <li>• Pale red in color (not too pale, not too red)</li>
        <li>• Moderately moist (not dry, not overly wet)</li>
        <li>• Thin, white, evenly distributed coating</li>
        <li>• Neither too swollen nor too thin</li>
        <li>• Moves freely and naturally</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
